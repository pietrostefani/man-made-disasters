Part 3 · Does Destruction Predict Socioeconomic Outcomes?

Linking physical damage to sentiment, income disruption & economic stress

Tip

A significant positive Moran’s I means residuals cluster — nearby households have similar unexplained outcomes. This suggests omitted spatially-varying variables (e.g. local aid delivery, social networks, infrastructure disruption).

Wrap-up

Key takeaways from the lab
  • Satellite data provides rapid, spatially consistent damage measurement — but misses facade damage, rubble clearance, and is blind to cloud cover.
  • Survey data captures lived experience and socioeconomic outcomes that remote sensing cannot see — but is costly, slow, and subject to recall and selection bias.
  • Triangulation reveals where sources agree (confidence) and where they diverge (new hypotheses).
  • Spatial autocorrelation in residuals reminds us that disasters don't affect households independently — local spillovers, aid patterns, and social networks matter.
  • Physical destruction predicts socioeconomic outcomes, but the relationship is mediated by pre-existing conditions — deprivation shapes who recovers and who doesn't.
