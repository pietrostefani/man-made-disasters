From Satellites to Areas

How we get from raw imagery to spatial damage metrics

How MAXAR imagery works

MAXAR operates a constellation of very-high-resolution (VHR) optical satellites — primarily WorldView-2, WorldView-3, and GeoEye-1 — capable of sub-metre resolution (30–50 cm panchromatic). After major disasters, MAXAR typically releases pre- and post-event imagery under their Open Data Program.

The change detection pipeline

Pre-event image   →  ┐
                      ├─ Co-registration → Differencing → Damage Index
Post-event image  →  ┘

Step 1: Image co-registration

Pre and post images must be aligned to sub-pixel accuracy. Any spatial offset creates false “change” signal.

Step 2: Spectral change detection

We compare spectral signatures before and after. Common approaches:

  • Normalised Difference Index on band ratios
  • Change Vector Analysis across multiple bands
  • Deep learning classifiers (CNN trained on labelled damage examples)

Step 3: Damage classification

The UNOSAT/JRC damage scale used in humanitarian response:

Class Description
1 No damage
2 Possible damage
3 Minor damage
4 Moderate damage
5 Severe damage
6 Destroyed

Step 4: Aggregation to areas

Individual building assessments are aggregated to grid cells or administrative units to enable statistical analysis and joining with survey/census data.

Limitations to keep in mind

Warning

Satellite damage assessment has known limitations:

  • Line-of-sight bias: Flat roofs look intact even with severe facade damage
  • Rubble vs. damage: Cleared rubble = no damage detected
  • Cloud cover: Optical imagery fails if post-event clouds present
  • Temporal gap: Imagery may be days–weeks post-event; recovery has begun
  • Building footprint quality: Aggregation quality depends on baseline building data

These limitations are exactly why we triangulate with survey data in Part 2 of the lab.

## Limitations to keep in mind

::: {.callout-warning}
**Satellite damage assessment has known limitations:**

- **Line-of-sight bias**: Flat roofs look intact even with severe facade damage

- **Temporal gap**: Imagery may be days–weeks post-event; recovery has begun
- **Building footprint quality**: Aggregation quality depends on baseline building data
:::

These limitations are *exactly why* we triangulate with survey data in Part 2 of the lab.
